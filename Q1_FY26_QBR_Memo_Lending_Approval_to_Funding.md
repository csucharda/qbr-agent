## Lending – Approval to Funding
*Reduce friction and improve conversion from approval to funding while increasing customer self-service.*

---

## 1.1 Executive Summary

The key priority for Q1 FY26 was to reduce friction across the approval-to-funding journey, with deliberate focus on three areas: simplifying document submission, removing manual review bottlenecks to reduce cycle time, and expanding digital self-service to reduce assisted support demand. Effort was concentrated on the highest drop-off moments in the journey — particularly document-related friction between approval and funding — where customer confusion and redundant process steps were suppressing conversion.

Digital self-service adoption continued to grow across the approval-to-funding journey, with 65% of customers now using digital channels to track status, submit documents, and resolve issues without assisted support (up from 58% at Q1 start). Funding conversion improved from 62% to 65%, document drop-off fell from 18% to 12%, and approval-to-funding cycle time was reduced from 19 to 18.5 days.

**Key learnings this quarter were:**

- **Clarity, not speed, drives customer behavior.** Removing redundant document requests and providing clearer instructions had a disproportionately large impact on both conversion (+3 pts) and NPS (+1.5 pts). The primary mechanism was reduced customer uncertainty — not process acceleration. This challenges the assumption that cycle time is the primary lever for funding conversion.
- **Reducing friction in a small number of high-impact moments delivers outsized gains.** Focused changes at the document submission stage drove improvements across funding rate, drop-off, and experience simultaneously — confirming the approval-to-funding journey is won or lost at discrete friction points, not across the whole flow.
- **Automation alone does not solve for complexity.** Partial automation of manual review reduced queue volume by ~15%, but complex cases still required full manual intervention. The structural constraint is case variability, not automation maturity — a distinction that should shape Q2 investment in early validation rather than further queue automation.
- **Proactive visibility reduces support demand.** Introducing proactive status tracking and clearer next steps drove a 10% reduction in call volume alongside a 7-point improvement in self-service rate — confirming that customers escalate when uncertain, not merely when dissatisfied.

**Our next opportunities are:**

- Scale document simplification to more complex customer scenarios, where drop-off and friction remain highest and Q1 gains have not yet reached.
- Further reduce approval-to-funding cycle time from 18.5 to ~18 days by expanding early validation and straight-through processing for standard cases.
- Extend self-service capability to edge cases and non-standard journeys, which currently fall back to assisted support and represent the next frontier of call volume reduction.

## 1.5 Risks

| Risk / Blocker | Ask for PBB ELT & Partners |
|---|---|
| **"Funding-ready" lacks a consistent, shared definition across teams and channels**, leading to inconsistent customer guidance, cautious decision-making, and duplicated indicators that create confusion rather than clarity. If unresolved, this will limit the impact of self-service improvements and create quality risk as we scale. | • Endorse a single, authoritative definition of "funding-ready" as the primary decision point across all channels and teams. • Support consistent application of this definition, including retiring familiar but duplicative indicators where they conflict. |
| **Improvement gains risk plateauing if edge cases are not addressed as a deliberate priority.** Q1 wins were concentrated in standard journeys. Edge cases — which carry higher manual effort, longer cycle time, and higher drop-off — are not yet within scope of existing initiatives and will become the binding constraint on further conversion and NPS improvement. | • Align on prioritising end-to-end journey optimisation over siloed, within-team efficiency gains. • Direct relevant domains (Credit, Approval, Digital) to treat edge-case resolution as a shared, cross-domain objective rather than a secondary backlog item. |
