# QBR Examples (Good vs Bad)

## What GOOD looks like

Example 1:
"Reducing friction in document submission had a disproportionate impact, improving conversion by +3 pts and NPS by +1.5 pts. The primary mechanism was reduced customer uncertainty — not process acceleration."

Example 2:
"While automation reduced review volume by ~15%, cycle time impact was limited due to edge-case complexity — highlighting that early validation, not downstream processing, is the primary lever."

Example 3:
"Proactive status tracking reduced inbound call volume by ~10%, confirming that visibility — not speed — drives customer confidence and behavior."

---

## What BAD looks like

Bad Example 1:
"We improved document submission and reduced cycle time."

Bad Example 2:
"Automation helped improve efficiency across the process."

Bad Example 3:
"We saw positive results across multiple initiatives."

---

## Rules to follow

- Always explain WHY something happened (cause → effect)
- Prefer insight over description
- Quantify impact whenever possible
- Highlight what changed behavior, not just process
- Avoid vague language
